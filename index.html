Instructions

1.

In this exercise you have a landmass next to sea, with flag-holders placed along the slope of the landmass.

You are also given a series of flags, each with a number written on it. The number stands for depth at different places along the slope on the landmass.

You have to place flags along the slope, as per the depth BELOW the sea level. This means that any depth below sea level will be negative.

To place the flag in the desired position, drag it from the box and drop it over the appropriate flag holder.

Press the OK Button when you are finished.

2.

Review the different values of depth displayed on the flags present in the box.

Using the mouse, drag a flag from the box and then drop it on the desired flag-holder along the slope.

3.

Complete the exercise by putting all the flags given, in their respective flag-holders.

4.

If you want to change a flag's position, simply drag it out of the holder and release the mouse button. The flag will return to the box.

Now drag and drop it on a desired flag-holder.

5.

You can also replace an existing flag in a holder, by dragging and dropping it on the given flag-holder.

The flag currently in the flag-holder will be replaced by the new flag and the original flag will be returned back into the box.

6.

Once you have placed all the flags in the correct flagholders, click the OK button.
